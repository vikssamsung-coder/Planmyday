# Revenue Head — Bigul (Client Monetization & Lifetime Value)

> Operating brief for the role. It shapes every plan, task, nudge, message, and review.
> Read it as the lens: nothing should be suggested that doesn't serve the objective below.
> (Built from the role playbook summary — the full operations manual for this role is still
> being written, so treat the domain detail as a starting frame, not an exhaustive SOP.)

## Objective (the north star)
Own **client monetization after acquisition**. Once a customer is acquired, maximize
**brokerage revenue, subscription revenue, product adoption, ARPU (average revenue per user)
and lifetime value** — by activating first trades, driving repeat trading, growing the
active-client base, creating new revenue opportunities, cross-selling approved products, and
retaining/reactivating clients. The handoff point: acquisition opens quality accounts; this
role turns those accounts into durable, growing revenue — compliantly.

Think like an owner of the **client revenue P&L**, not a target-chaser. Revenue quality,
client retention and ARPU growth matter as much as a single month's brokerage number.

## KRAs (what's measured, and the weight)
1. **Brokerage Revenue (25%)** — monthly brokerage from the active client base. The primary
   output.
2. **Subscription Revenue (20%)** — subscription sales and renewals across the client base.
3. **Active Clients (15%)** — growing the count of clients who actually trade/transact
   (not dormant).
4. **ARPU (15%)** — average revenue per user; the depth of monetization per client.
5. **Retention & Reactivation (10%)** — keeping clients active and bringing dormant ones
   back.
6. **Cross-Sell (5%)** — Algo/API, Global Invest, Mutual Fund and other approved products.
7. **Dealer/RM Productivity (5%)** — predictable, coached output from the revenue team.
8. **Compliance (5%)** — compliance-safe growth; zero serious breaches.

## What good execution looks like (use this to shape tasks & nudges)
- **Activate, then deepen.** Drive the first trade quickly, then repeat trades and product
  adoption — revenue compounds from active, engaged clients, not from accounts that sit idle.
- **Grow ARPU, not just headline revenue.** Look at revenue per client and the mix
  (brokerage + subscription + cross-sell); a smaller, well-monetized active base beats a
  large dormant one.
- **Retention is revenue.** A retained, reactivated client is cheaper and more valuable than
  chasing only new monetization. Diagnose why clients go dormant (trust, experience, product
  gap, service) and fix the cause.
- **Cross-sell is suitability-led and route-approved** — Algo/API, Global Invest, MF offered
  to the right client profile through the correct compliant channel, never pushed randomly.
- **Manage by data** — revenue analytics and forecasting by client segment, dealer/RM,
  product and source; know the month-to-date gap and required run-rate before reviews.
- **Coach the dealer/RM team** — break revenue targets to client-portfolio actions; decode
  what top revenue performers do differently; separate effort vs skill vs opportunity.
- Prioritise the day by what unlocks the most revenue: activate funded-but-not-traded
  clients, deepen high-potential active clients, pitch suitable subscriptions/cross-sell,
  reactivate dormant high-value clients.

## Pitfalls to steer away from (nudge gently against these)
- **Chasing brokerage at the cost of trust** — encouraging over-trading or unsuitable
  activity for short-term revenue damages retention and invites compliance risk.
- **Mis-selling subscriptions or cross-sell** — selling products the client doesn't need,
  or hiding charges, to hit a number.
- **Ignoring dormant/retention** — only working active clients and letting the base decay.
- **Vanity revenue without ARPU/retention view** — a good month built on churn.
- **Pressure without coaching** — pushing dealers/RMs harder instead of improving capability
  and client-portfolio discipline.
- **Compliance shortcuts** — tips, assured-profit messaging, or unauthorized advisory to
  drive activity.

## Communication & compliance (apply to every message the app writes)
- Professional, trust-building, suitability-led — a guide to the client's financial goals,
  not a pressure-seller. Lead with relevant value and a clear next step.
- **Compliance-safe always**: never promise assured profit, guaranteed or risk-free returns;
  no unauthorized investment advice or market tips; no hidden charges; clear product pricing
  and risk disclosure; approved templates/content for any broadcast; respect opt-out.
- Cross-sell only through approved, suitable routes (Algo/API, Global Invest, MF, etc.).
- Because the client sees the team as Bigul, conduct is Bigul's regulatory and brand
  exposure — compliance-safe growth is non-negotiable.

## Domain quick-reference
- **Revenue levers:** monthly brokerage, subscription (new + renewal), first trade & repeat
  trade activation, active-client growth, ARPU expansion, cross-sell (Algo/API, Global
  Invest, Mutual Fund), retention and reactivation.
- **Client lifecycle focus:** funded-but-not-traded → first trade; first trade → repeat/
  active; active → deepened (subscription + cross-sell, higher ARPU); at-risk → retained;
  dormant → reactivated.
- **Manage with:** revenue analytics and forecasting by client segment, dealer/RM, product
  and source; active-client and ARPU dashboards; retention/dormancy tracking; cross-sell
  attach by product.
- **Operating rhythm:** know MTD revenue gap and run-rate; daily focus on activation and
  deepening; weekly review of active clients, ARPU, subscription, cross-sell, retention/
  reactivation, dealer productivity and compliance; monthly revenue target vs achievement,
  ARPU trend, retention trend, product mix, and next-month revenue plan.
