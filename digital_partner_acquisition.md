# Digital Partner Growth & Activation Manager — Bigul

> Operating brief for the role. It shapes every plan, task, nudge, message, and review.
> Read it as the lens: nothing should be suggested that doesn't serve the objective below.
> (Role formerly called "Digital Partner Acquisition / Recreation Manager" — the real job is
> the full partner lifecycle and revenue, not just lead conversion.)

## Objective (the north star)
Acquire, activate, engage, and grow partners acquired primarily through **digital channels**
(and self-sourced prospecting), so that partners **regularly contribute new account openings
and revenue**. The role begins at digital lead conversion and does **not** end at
registration — its true purpose is to ensure each acquired partner is onboarded, trained,
engaged, activated, and converted into a regular business-producing partner. Judge the role
on **lifecycle conversion** (lead → register → activate → accounts → revenue), never on lead
calling or registration count alone — a large registered base is worthless if partners
aren't activated, opening accounts, or producing revenue.

This role works **through teams** — marketing (lead gen), telecalling (qualification +
registration follow-up), and a relationship team (onboarding, activation, engagement). The
manager is **accountable for the outcomes**, **responsible for process quality and targets**
through those teams, and **dependent on** operations/product/compliance for enablement.
Coordinate proactively; own the result.

## The four targets & KRAs (what's measured, and the weight)
1. **New Partner Acquisition (20%)** — new registered partners; digital lead-to-registration
   conversion; **self-sourced** registrations; qualified prospect pipeline. (Track marketing
   leads vs self-sourced separately to see channel productivity.)
2. **Partner Activation (25%)** — activation rate; onboarding completion; first valid
   business action within 15/30 days; reduction of inactive registered partners.
3. **New Account Acquisition (25%)** — accounts opened through partners; accounts per active
   partner; partner-lead-to-account conversion; account-opening TAT.
4. **Revenue Generation (30%)** — monthly revenue & target achievement; **revenue per active
   partner**; revenue from newly activated partners; repeat revenue. (Revenue carries the
   highest weight — the role's ultimate value is business output, not registration.)

## Partner lifecycle (each status = a clear next action)
Lead (qualify, assign calling, track) → Qualified lead (share registration process, follow
up to close) → **Registered partner** (trigger onboarding call + training immediately — this
is the start, not the finish) → Onboarded (give first 7-day action plan, track first
business action) → **Activated** (first valid business action in timeline → move to regular
engagement + account support) → Revenue-generating (nurture, grow, cross-sell, business
reviews) → Dormant (reactivation call/campaign/refresh, reason analysis, retention attempt).

## Daily / weekly / monthly rhythm (run it predictably so nothing leaks)
- **Daily:** review new leads, registration status, onboarding-pending list, activation-
  pending list, account-opening cases, revenue run-rate, escalations → produce a clear
  action list for telecalling, relationship team, and support functions. Monitor relationship-
  team calls, partner follow-ups, onboarding calls, and query resolution.
- **Weekly:** funnel review (leads → registered → activated → accounts → revenue) with
  conversion-gap analysis; partner segmentation (high-potential / dormant / low-output);
  lead-quality + campaign-source feedback to marketing.
- **Monthly:** revenue review by partner, cohort, source, campaign; data cleanup + dormant-
  list validation; plan acquisition/activation/account/revenue campaigns (growth calendar).

## What good execution looks like (use this to shape tasks & nudges)
- **Registration is the start, not the finish.** The gap between registration and revenue is
  where this role lives — run onboarding within TAT, give every new partner a **first 7-day
  action plan**, and chase the first business action.
- **Run the partner journey as a measurable funnel** — every stage has an owner, a conversion
  metric, and a next action, so no partner gets stuck between register → onboard → activate →
  produce.
- **Segment partners and vary the cadence** — new registered (Day 0/2/7/15/30), onboarded-but-
  inactive (2–3 touches/week until activation), activated-low-output (weekly productivity
  coaching), active revenue partner (weekly/fortnightly review + recognition), high-potential
  (priority engagement), dormant (reactivation campaign). One cadence for all wastes effort.
- **Link every activity to accounts and revenue** — calls, follow-ups, training and campaigns
  are inputs; they only matter if they move account openings and revenue per active partner.
- **Build a self-sourced pipeline** — don't depend only on marketing leads; mine approved
  public sources / directories / referrals / networks, categorize prospects, and track
  self-sourced conversion separately.
- **Manage by data** — keep a clean daily dashboard (leads, registrations, onboarding,
  activation, accounts, revenue, pending actions); use conversion gaps to fix scripts,
  campaigns and communication; coach the relationship/telecalling teams on quality,
  conversion and TAT — not just volume.
- Prioritise the day by what unlocks the most progress: act on fresh/aged leads before they
  go cold, clear the onboarding-pending list, unstick partners before their first business
  action, push account-opening cases through, and give high-potential partners focused
  attention.

## Pitfalls to steer away from (nudge gently against these)
- **Registrations without activation** — the core failure; partner base grows, revenue
  doesn't. Mandatory onboarding call + first-7-day plan + activation dashboard.
- **High lead volume but low contact rate** — campaign spend wasted. Daily lead-ageing
  review, telecalling SLA, missed-lead escalation.
- **Weak product/process understanding** — partner confusion → low confidence, poor
  conversion. Simple training modules, FAQs, process checklist, refreshers.
- **Unclear ownership between teams** — leads/partners fall between marketing, calling and
  relationship teams. Stage-wise ownership + dashboard + weekly review.
- **Poor data quality / fake dispositions** — management can't trust the reports. CRM hygiene
  score, mandatory dispositions, status audits.
- **Revenue concentrated in a few partners** — fragile. Develop mid-tier partners, activate
  new cohorts, track revenue distribution.
- **Dormant partner build-up** — acquisition cost rises, productivity falls. Dormancy
  triggers + reactivation campaigns + periodic cleanup.
- **Counting activity over output** — measuring calls/registrations instead of activated
  partners, accounts and revenue.

## Communication & compliance (apply to every message the app writes)
- Clear, persuasive, value-led — motivate partners and explain the opportunity (earning
  model, product, process, first steps), always with a next action.
- **Compliance-safe always**: market prospecting, data usage and outreach must use **approved
  sources** and follow company communication policy — **no unapproved scraping, data misuse,
  or non-compliant outreach**. No assured-profit/guaranteed-return claims; respect consent and
  opt-out; use approved templates for WhatsApp/email/messaging where required.
- Partner registration, onboarding and documentation follow approved process and controls.

## Domain quick-reference
- **Lead sources:** marketing-generated (campaigns, forms, landing pages, online inquiries,
  ads, referral pages) + **self-sourced** (industry directories, public listings, business
  networks, referrals, local markets, approved digital platforms). Track the two separately.
- **Onboarding call agenda:** verify profile → explain company/product/opportunity/earning
  model → explain account-opening process, eligibility, documentation, common rejection
  reasons → train first customer pitch + objection handling → share support/escalation/CRM
  channels → set first 7-day action plan with target customers, expected leads, follow-up
  date, milestone.
- **Activation levers:** onboarding scripts + FAQs for relationship-team consistency; first-
  7-day plan for every new partner; short repeatable product/process training modules;
  approved WhatsApp/email communication; partner-wise nudges on account/revenue gaps;
  recognition (first account, monthly top partners); reactivation offers + refresh training
  for dormant partners.
- **Core metrics:** lead contact rate, lead-to-registration conversion, registration TAT,
  onboarding completion rate, activation rate, time to activation, account openings, accounts
  per active partner, revenue achievement, revenue per active partner, dormant-partner ratio,
  data-hygiene score.
- **Team interfaces:** marketing (give lead-quality + campaign-conversion feedback),
  telecalling (monitor contact rate, conversion, pending leads, call quality), relationship
  team (set cadence, scripts, targets, escalation), operations (escalate account/activation
  bottlenecks), product/training (push updated knowledge), compliance (keep prospecting +
  outreach + onboarding compliant), CRM/MIS (data hygiene + funnel tracking).
