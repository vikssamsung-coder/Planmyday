# Digital Partner Acquisition Manager — Bigul (Build the partner engine, run the team, close the big dealers yourself)

> Operating brief and daily lens for the Digital Partner Acquisition Manager (a.k.a. Digital
> Partner Growth & Activation Manager). It shapes every plan, task, nudge, message, and
> review. Read it as the lens: nothing should be suggested that doesn't serve the objective
> below. This is a LEADER's brief — it covers running the department, managing the team, AND
> the manager's own selling effort (the big dealers).

## Objective (the north star)
Acquire, activate, engage, and grow a scalable network of digital partners (sub-brokers /
authorised persons, referral partners, IFAs, finance influencers/affiliates, ex-broking
professionals, corporate connectors) acquired through **digital channels and self-sourced
prospecting**, so partners **consistently generate new KYC-complete, funded trading accounts
and revenue** on the Bigul platform. The role begins at digital lead conversion and does
**not** end at registration — its purpose is to turn each partner into a regular business-
producing partner. Judge it on **lifecycle conversion** (lead → register → activate →
accounts → revenue), never on registration count alone — a big registered base is worthless
if partners aren't activated, opening funded accounts, or producing revenue.

## What the manager does personally vs. through the team (the player-coach split)
This is a leadership role that delivers **through teams** — marketing (lead gen), telecalling
(qualification + registration follow-up), and a relationship team (onboarding, activation,
day-to-day servicing) — AND through the manager's **own high-value acquisition + senior
relationship**.

- **A "BIG DEALER" is defined by a hard bar:** a partner with **more than 500 client accounts
  with another broker**, OR doing **more than ₹1 lakh/month in revenue**. (Either threshold
  qualifies.) These are the partners worth the manager's personal time.
- **Manager's personal ownership = ACQUIRE + LEVEL-2 RELATIONSHIP; the team services.** For big
  dealers, the manager **personally prospects, pitches, closes, and opens** the partner — these
  prospects expect a senior counterpart and convert on direct senior attention, so they are
  never left in a junior queue. After opening, **day-to-day servicing is handled by the team**,
  but the **manager keeps a periodic Level-2 relationship** — speaking to the big-dealer partner
  directly on a regular (e.g. periodic check-in) basis to retain trust, review business, and
  catch issues early. So: **manager opens it and stays the senior relationship touchpoint; the
  team runs the servicing underneath.**
- **Volume partners run fully through the team.** Marketing/self-sourced leads flow to
  telecalling for qualification and registration follow-up, then to the relationship team for
  onboarding, activation and servicing — with the manager owning **process quality, targets,
  coaching, and escalations**, not every individual call.
- The manager is **accountable for all outcomes**, **responsible for process quality and
  targets** through the teams, **personally responsible for acquiring big dealers and holding
  their Level-2 relationship**, and **dependent on** operations/product/compliance for
  enablement. Coordinate proactively; own the result.

## Daily operating rhythm (run the day like a control room)
**Morning (08:00–10:00) — set the board.** Review overnight leads/enquiries/inbound
partnership requests, prioritise by intent. Clear yesterday's follow-up SLA breaches (any open
>24 hr) before new outreach. Pull the dashboard: pipeline stage counts, activation rate,
funded accounts via partners (T-1), revenue run-rate, escalations → produce the day's action
list for telecalling + relationship teams. Set today's three non-negotiables (one acquisition,
one activation, one big-dealer / relationship-deepening move).

**Mid-morning (10:00–13:00) — prospect & pitch.** Manager's own outreach block to **big-dealer
prospects** (existing AP/sub-brokers with a book, corporate connectors, high-reach IFAs/
creators) — one clear CTA per conversation, led by Bigul's value props (technology edge,
payout structure, support ecosystem). Monitor the team's calling on volume leads. Log every
interaction in CRM immediately; tag stage.

**Afternoon (13:00–16:00) — activate & support.** Check which recently onboarded partners
haven't referred their first funded client → trigger activation (quick-start kit, 1:1 call,
demo). Ensure the relationship team is servicing onboarded partners (including big dealers the
manager opened); the manager makes **periodic Level-2 relationship calls to big-dealer
partners** to review business and catch issues early. Resolve/escalate partner queries (payout,
platform how-to, compliance) within 24 hrs.

**Late afternoon (16:00–18:00) — hygiene, team review & planning.** Update CRM, move stages,
flag stalled deals (no response >72 hrs = re-engage or disqualify). Review team productivity
(calls, connects, follow-ups, activation closures); coach the bottom performer. Send pending
agreements / portal access / welcome kits. Build tomorrow's priority prospect list and one
market/product update worth sharing with the partner network.

## If-then decision rules (use these to shape tasks & nudges)
| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| New inbound partner enquiry | Respond within **2 hours**; qualify on client-base size, digital reach, past financial-products experience. **If big-dealer profile (>500 accounts elsewhere or >₹1 lakh/month revenue) → manager acquires it personally**, else route to the team. |
| Fresh marketing/self-sourced lead, high-intent | Allocate to best telecalling RM same day; first attempt + KYC/registration CTA; track same-day outcome. |
| Partner onboarded but no referral after 14 days | Schedule activation call; share quick-start kit + first-referral incentive reminder. |
| Partner onboarded but no referral after 30 days | Mark "at-risk"; offer personalised co-marketing support or re-assess fit. |
| Prospect goes silent after 2 follow-ups | Send a value-add message (market insight / Bigul feature) — not a pitch; wait 5 days, then nurture or close. |
| Partner raises payout/compliance query | Resolve or escalate to ops/compliance within **24 hrs**; follow up to confirm closure. |
| Referred client completes KYC but doesn't fund | Alert the partner; give a ready re-engagement template (no guaranteed-return language). |
| High-performing partner (≥5 funded clients/month) or any big dealer (>500 accts elsewhere / >₹1 lakh/month) | **Manager's** periodic Level-2 relationship call (not just the team); priority support, recognition, tier upgrade. |
| Source/campaign high volume but low conversion for 3 cycles | Prepare data-backed feedback to marketing; recommend pause / retarget / creative change. |
| Team RM conversion low but activity high | Audit calls + pitch quality; **coach, don't just pressure**. |
| Compliance-risk language found in any partner comms | Stop the template, retrain, escalate if serious, audit past communication. |

## Partner lifecycle (each stage = psychology, treatment, KPI)
- **Identified (cold prospect).** Unaware/indifferent → lead with credibility + differentiation
  (technology, payouts, brand); short curiosity-driven outreach; one CTA ("book a 15-min intro
  call"). KPI: response/connection rate. *(Big dealers: manager's own outreach.)*
- **Engaged (conversation started).** Curious, comparing existing affiliations → personalise to
  their audience type; share partner proof points; answer exclusivity/effort/earnings
  objections honestly. KPI: discovery-call completion.
- **Pitched (proposal/demo shared).** Interested, needs conviction + simplicity → partner-portal
  demo; clarify payout mechanics; remove onboarding friction; one CTA ("complete your partner
  KYC today"). KPI: agreement sign-up rate; pitch-to-signup time.
- **Onboarded (agreement signed, not yet active).** Committed but unsure how to start —
  **highest activation risk** → structured 7-day activation plan; first-referral goal; easy
  support; celebrate the first referral loudly. KPI: time to first funded referral; % activated
  within 30 days.
- **Active (referring regularly).** Invested; motivated by recognition/earnings/ease → regular
  performance updates; timely payouts; co-marketing assets; tier upsell. KPI: funded referrals
  per partner per month; funded-conversion rate.
- **Dormant (no referral 30+ days).** Disengaged / hit friction → re-engagement call; understand
  blockers; refresh with new product/campaign hook; after two attempts move to inactive pool,
  re-surface quarterly. KPI: re-activation rate.

## What good execution looks like
- **Registration is the start, not the finish** — onboarding within TAT, a first-7-day plan for
  every new partner, and a chased first funded referral. A signed agreement is not a win; the
  first funded referral is.
- **The manager personally acquires big dealers and holds their Level-2 relationship** — big
  dealers (>500 accounts elsewhere or >₹1 lakh/month revenue) are always prospected, pitched
  and opened by the manager, then **serviced by the team** while the manager keeps a periodic
  direct relationship; never left to a junior queue for acquisition.
- **Run the journey as a measurable funnel** — every stage has an owner, a conversion metric,
  and a next action; no partner gets stuck between register → onboard → activate → produce.
- **Pipeline always has ≥30 qualified prospects across stages**; no stage empty; **≥2 new
  partner prospects added every working day** (manager + team).
- **Segment and vary cadence** — new registered (Day 0/2/7/15/30), onboarded-but-inactive (2–3
  touches/week until activation), activated-low-output (weekly coaching), active (weekly/
  fortnightly review + recognition), **big dealer / high-potential (team services day-to-day +
  manager's periodic Level-2 relationship call)**, dormant (reactivation campaign). One cadence
  for all wastes effort.
- **Link every activity to funded accounts and revenue** — calls, follow-ups, training and
  campaigns are inputs; they matter only if they move funded accounts and revenue per active
  partner.
- **Build a self-sourced pipeline** tracked separately from marketing leads (approved sources/
  directories/referrals/networks).
- **Manage by data** — clean same-day CRM; no verbal promise unlogged; partner queries never
  open beyond 24 hrs; use conversion gaps to fix scripts/campaigns/communication and to coach
  the team on quality, conversion and TAT — not just volume.
- **Diversify outreach** — if calls aren't landing, test LinkedIn, WhatsApp, email, community
  groups.

## Team leadership (run the department through people)
- Break the monthly target → daily → RM-level action; give each RM a lead portfolio (fresh /
  KYC-pending / account-pending / activation / aged), not a random list.
- Separate **effort vs skill vs opportunity**: high effort + low result → coach; low effort →
  accountability; low opportunity → fix lead allocation. Decode what top performers do
  differently (pitch, timing, follow-up, objection handling) and spread it.
- Give underperformers **specific** corrective plans ("improve Day-0 first attempt / KYC
  objection handling / follow-up compliance"), not "convert more".
- Set the relationship/telecalling team's scripts, cadence, QA and escalation discipline; run
  daily floor focus and weekly reviews; recognise first accounts and top partners.

## Communication & compliance (apply to every message the app writes)
- **One CTA per message** — every partner/prospect communication ends with exactly one clear
  next step.
- **No guaranteed-return language** — never "assured income," "guaranteed profit," "risk-free
  earnings," "fixed returns," or similar, in any partner- or client-facing message.
- **SEBI / exchange compliance** — partner materials and referral communications must align with
  SEBI sub-broker / authorised-person guidelines; when in doubt, escalate to compliance before
  sending. Co-created partner content must carry required investment-risk disclosures.
- **Factual payout representation** — share only current, approved partner-programme terms.
- **Approved sources & data privacy** — self-sourced prospecting uses **approved sources only**
  (no unapproved scraping/data misuse/non-compliant outreach); prospect/partner data is
  confidential; respect consent and opt-out; use approved templates for WhatsApp/email/
  messaging where required.
- **Professional tone** — concise, respectful, jargon-light; Bigul as a tech-forward,
  trustworthy broking platform. Lead with partner economics, support and trust — not pressure.
