# Role Brief — Head, Quality, Training & Process Excellence

**Use this brief to plan the day for a Quality & Training Head in a stock broking business. Every task on the plan must ladder to one of the goals below. If it doesn't, it gets parked, deferred, delegated, or dropped — and you say why.**

---

## Who this role is

Owns the quality + training + process-excellence operating system across Sales, Account Opening, Activation, Dealing, Customer Care, Service, and Email/Ticket teams — voice and non-voice. This is a **business-protection and capability-building** role, not a call-scoring desk. The job is to convert audit insight into coaching, SOP fixes, and AI/Auto-QA improvements that measurably reduce defects, compliance risk, and revenue leakage.

**One-line test for any task:** does it improve quality, train capability, fix a process gap, govern the AI, or protect against a compliance/revenue risk? If not, it isn't this role's work today.

---

## North-star goals (everything ladders to these)

1. **Consistent voice + non-voice quality** across all covered teams — fewer repeat defects, wrong pitches, mis-sells, missing steps, premature ticket closures.
2. **Lower compliance + revenue leakage** — no assured-return language, no unauthorized trading, clean KYC guidance, correct charges, proper complaint handling.
3. **Training that moves the needle** — low performers improve, repeat errors drop, certifications hold before deployment on sensitive work.
4. **AI/Auto-QA stays trustworthy** — manual-AI calibration maintained, fatal errors caught, ZipTeam outputs validated not just consumed.
5. **Closed-loop process improvement** — recurring gaps get an owner, an action, a due date, and a re-audit that proves the defect reduced.

A day with no movement on goals 1–5 is a busy day, not a productive one.

---

## KRA weights (use these to rank what matters when the day is overloaded)

| KRA | Weight | The goal it serves |
|---|---|---|
| Quality governance & improvement | 25% | Goals 1, 2 |
| Training effectiveness | 20% | Goal 3 |
| Audit coverage, accuracy & closure | 15% | Goal 1 |
| Process improvement & risk reduction | 15% | Goals 2, 5 |
| AI/Auto-QA & ZipTeam governance | 10% | Goal 4 |
| Reporting & stakeholder management | 10% | All |
| Team leadership & capability building | 5% | Goal 3 |

When two tasks compete, the higher-weight KRA usually wins — unless a fatal-error escalation is live (see below), which always jumps the queue.

---

## Horizon split — "achieve today" vs "build tomorrow"

The day must contain both. All delivery and no building means the system never improves; all building and no delivery means nothing closes.

**`Today` (delivers now):**
- QA team huddle, audit-completion review, pending-task closure
- Reviewing critical/escalated calls personally
- Sharing daily quality insights + recommended actions with Ops
- AI vs manual calibration check on the day's sample
- Escalating any live fatal error immediately

**`Build` (compounds toward near-future goals):**
- Redesigning a department scorecard or fatal-error definition
- Building a training module / TNI from a recurring defect trend
- Driving a CAPA to closure (owner, action, due date)
- Calibration sessions to tighten QA consistency
- Improving an AI taxonomy / parameter so future audits get sharper

---

## Recurring obligations (these must appear or be consciously skipped — never silently dropped)

**Daily:** review QA/trainer task completion · run the quality huddle · ensure end-to-end closure of open audit/feedback/report/process actions · review audit completion across departments · monitor dashboards · review AI audits vs manual · validate AI insights (intent, objection, VOC) · review ZipTeam outputs + feedback · meet Ops on priorities/campaigns · share actionable insights with Ops.

**Weekly:** department-wise audit performance + feedback closure · process-gap review → SOP/parameter recommendations · AI/ZipTeam enhancement follow-up.

**Monthly:** management dashboard (trend, top-10 gaps, business impact, AI progress, training effectiveness, CAPA closure) · next-month quality + training plan.

If a daily obligation is being skipped repeatedly, that's goal-drift — name it.

---

## Numbers that drive "Today's numbers"

Track movement, not raw counts. The metrics that matter:
- **Quality score** (overall + by department)
- **Fatal-error rate**
- **Repeat-defect rate** (same parameter, same agent, after feedback)
- **Feedback closure %** within TAT
- **Ticket reopen rate**
- **AI/manual variance**
- **Training impact** (pre/post, repeat-error reduction)
- **CAPA closure %**
- **Audit plan adherence %**

A daily target should map to one of these. "Reviewed 20 calls" is not a goal; "cut dealing-call order-evidence defects this week" is.

---

## Fatal errors / red flags — these jump the queue regardless of plan

Any of these surfaces → escalate immediately, preserve evidence, and the day reprioritizes around it:
- Assured-return / profit-guarantee / misleading-earnings language
- Unauthorized trading or order placed without clear client instruction
- Customer data / privacy breach
- Wrong brokerage/charges or regulatory info causing customer loss
- Complaint not recognized or prematurely closed
- Abusive / disrespectful agent behavior
- Ticket/email closed without real resolution or evidence

These are non-negotiable interrupts. A live fatal error outranks the planned agenda.

---

## Stakeholders to engage (quality only lands when these own the action)

Sales Head · Dealing Head · Customer Care/Service Head · Operations Head · Compliance/Risk · Technology/CRM/AI vendor (ZipTeam) · HR/L&D · Management. A recommendation with no named owner and due date is not closed — it's parked.

---

## Goal-drift traps specific to this role (catch and name these)

- **Audit volume as the goal.** Completing audits feels productive but only counts if it surfaces a defect that gets coached or fixed. Audits with no downstream action = busywork.
- **Generic training calendars.** Soft-skills sessions unlinked to actual QA trends don't move repeat-defect rates. Training must trace to a measured gap.
- **Consuming AI dashboards instead of governing them.** Reading ZipTeam output is not the same as validating it. If no calibration or correction came out of it, the AI-governance goal didn't move.
- **Feedback given, closure never checked.** Coaching without a re-audit is an open loop. The loop closes when the defect drops in a later audit.
- **Reporting for its own sake.** A dashboard that lists counts but names no risk, trend, or required action is activity, not insight.

When 2–3 tasks fill the day but none of them move a metric in "Today's numbers," flag it: *"These keep the day full but don't reduce a defect, improve a score, or close a gap. Should they really be today?"*

---

## How to apply the Gate for this role

For each candidate task, force the link:
- **Which goal (1–5) and KRA does it serve?** No link → fails the Gate.
- **Horizon — `Today` or `Build`?** Name it so the day isn't all one or the other.
- **Is a fatal error live?** If yes, it outranks everything planned.
- **Does it close a loop or open one?** Prefer tasks that close (re-audit, CAPA closure, certification) over tasks that only generate more to track.

A tight plan tied to quality-score movement, defect reduction, and gap closure beats a long plan of audits and reports that feel thorough but move nothing.
