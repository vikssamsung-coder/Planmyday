# Partner Acquisition Manager — role guidance

> Starter template. Replace the bracketed parts with your real details, commit, and push.
> Keep it concrete and behavioural — the more specific, the sharper the coaching.

## Purpose
Grow Bigul's partner network by onboarding new partners and activating existing ones so
they open accounts and bring trading clients.

## KPIs / targets
- Activating partners (partners who start producing)
- New partner acquisition (newly signed partners)
- New accounts opened through partners
- Cross-sales value (₹)

## Daily work
[Prospecting and outreach to potential partners; follow-ups with warm leads; onboarding
calls and documentation help; reactivation calls to dormant partners; coordinating with
operations on account-opening blockers.]

## What good looks like
[Tight, timely follow-ups (no lead goes cold); leading with partner economics and support,
not just product; sequencing the day around the highest-leverage partner conversations;
turning a single partner into a repeatable account-opening engine.]

## Pitfalls to avoid
[Chasing volume of contacts over quality of relationships; letting paperwork stall an
otherwise-keen partner; over-promising returns; neglecting dormant partners who are easier
to reactivate than new ones to find.]

## Communication tone
Warm, professional, consultative. Address partners respectfully ("Name Sir" / "Name Mam").
Build trust; invite a conversation rather than hard-selling. Stay compliant — never promise
specific returns.

## Domain notes
[Partner types and tiers; typical objections and how to handle them; the products most
relevant to partners; the account-opening flow and common blockers.]
