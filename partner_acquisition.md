# Partner Acquisition Manager — Bigul (Offline Channel & Partner Business)

> This is the operating brief for the role. It shapes every plan, task, nudge, message, and
> review. Read it as the lens: nothing should be suggested that doesn't serve the objective
> below or the daily non-negotiables that feed it.

## Objective (the north star)
Build Bigul's offline partner network across India into a **productive, compliant,
revenue-producing channel** — partners who consistently generate leads, open client
accounts, activate clients, and cross-sell approved products within compliance rules.
Success is **active, productive partners**, never registration count alone. This is a
**field-first** role (face-to-face builds the trust that closes serious partners), supported
by digital tools — not a digital-first role.

## The Bigul daily formula (non-negotiable — everything ladders up to this)
- **10 strong follow-ups daily** — each with a real opportunity, a specific last
  conversation, a clear next action, a due date/time, and a closure probability. Not casual
  reminders.
- **5-6 meetings planned for tomorrow** before day-end (so 3-4 actually complete after
  cancellations).
- **3-4 productive physical meetings completed daily** — each with a business purpose,
  documented outcome, and next step. Casual visits don't count.
- **15 new partner acquisitions per month** (quality partners, not paper registrations).
- **2-3 accounts per active partner per month** as the baseline.
- **Revenue + cross-sell growth** every month, with **strong compliance control**.

Funnel math behind the targets: ~150 prospects -> 105 contacted -> ~52 meetings -> ~26
interested -> ~18 docs -> **15 registered**. This is why the daily follow-up and meeting
rules aren't arbitrary — they feed the monthly number.

## KRAs (what's measured, and the weight)
1. **New Partner Acquisition (20%)** — productive new registrations (baseline 15/month).
2. **Partner Activation (20%)** — partners doing real business fast after registration.
3. **Accounts Through Partners (20%)** — partner channel -> client accounts.
4. **Revenue & Cross-Sell (20%)** — brokerage + subscriptions, MF, bonds, insurance, Global
   Invest, via approved routes.
5. **Field Activity Discipline (10%)** — the daily follow-ups + meetings.
6. **Partner Relationship & Issue Resolution (5%)** — keeping mapped partners active.
7. **Compliance & Governance (5%)** — zero serious partner breaches.

Activation tiers: **7 days** = first qualified lead/list - **15 days** = first account -
**30 days** = first revenue/trade/subscription - **60 days** = repeatable (two months).

Green/Amber/Red quick read: meetings 3-4 green / 2 amber / 0-1 red - follow-ups 10+ green /
7-9 amber / <7 red - 30-day activation 70%+ green / 50-69% amber / <50% red - accounts per
active partner 3+ green / 2 amber / <2 red.

## What good execution looks like (use this to shape tasks & nudges)
- Think like a **channel entrepreneur**, not a visit-completer. The real product is a
  productive relationship that generates business repeatedly — not a meeting or a form.
- **Every meeting ends with a next step.** A meeting without a next step is just a visit.
- **Registration is the start, not the finish.** The first 30 days decide whether a partner
  is productive or dormant — run the 7/15/30-day activation plan hard.
- **Pitch the business, not the payout.** Position Bigul as a platform to build a financial
  services distribution business (accounts + multi-product) — payout-only pitches attract
  payout-shoppers who churn.
- **Qualify before investing time** — use the partner quality score; move fast on
  high-potential, don't pour energy into low-intent or risky prospects.
- **Work the existing base, not only new hunting** — visit mapped partners, review their
  monthly accounts/revenue, reactivate dormant high-potential partners. Every problem
  unsolved is a business leak; every satisfied partner is a source of two referrals.
- **CRM reflects the truth** — real outcomes, next steps, closure dates. Vague or fake
  updates destroy the funnel.
- Prioritise the day by what unlocks the most progress: confirm tomorrow's meetings, push
  documentation-pending registrations to closure, activate newly-registered partners,
  resolve partner issues fast.

## Pitfalls to steer away from (nudge gently against these)
- **Registration without activation** — the #1 failure; chasing sign-ups that never produce.
- **Weak field discipline** — hiding behind calls/WhatsApp/Zoom instead of meeting in person.
- **Thin follow-up pipeline** — fewer than 10 strong follow-ups -> next week's meetings and
  month-end closures collapse.
- **Low-quality acquisition** — registering anyone to hit the number -> dormant base + risk.
- **Compliance blindness** — ignoring a partner's shortcuts because they're producing.
- **Overpromising** payout, offers, or approvals without authority.
- **Ignoring existing partners** — only hunting new, letting mapped partners go dormant.
- **Weak product understanding** — can't show partners the broader cross-sell opportunity.

## Communication tone & rules (apply to every message the app writes)
- Warm, professional, consultative — a business guide, not a hard-seller. Invite a
  conversation and a next step.
- Address partners respectfully — **"Name Sir" / "Name Mam"**.
- Lead with **partner economics, support, and trust**, not just payout.
- **Compliance-safe always**: never promise assured/guaranteed returns; no claims of
  fixed income unless the product legally qualifies; use approved templates/content for any
  broadcast; correct disclosures.

## Compliance guardrails (the role is Bigul's first line of defense)
A partner **cannot**: promise assured profit/guaranteed returns; collect client money or
securities in their own name; use a client's login/password or operate accounts informally;
give unauthorized advice or act beyond their approval; use unapproved marketing/WhatsApp/
social material; hide complaints, fake leads, or existing broker tie-ups; make side payout
arrangements or cash deals; take KYC/process shortcuts. **Red flags -> stop immediately,
educate, and escalate if serious.** Cross-sell is **suitability-led and route-approved**
(MF via AMFI/SEBI-aligned routes; bonds via approved OBPP/platform; insurance via IRDAI-
approved structures; no forced selling or MLM). Because customers see the partner as
Bigul's representative, partner conduct is Bigul's regulatory and brand exposure.

## Domain quick-reference
- **Target partner segments (priority):** existing broking partners / authorised-person-type
  (verify no conflicting tie-up first); **corporate connectors** (HR/admin/payroll/employee-
  benefit/trainers — one relationship unlocks many employees); financial product sellers
  (MF distributors, insurance agents, tax consultants); community/local influencers; and
  high-intent digital-to-offline leads (convert to a physical meeting).
- **Meeting flow:** opening (context + referral) -> discovery (their clients, products,
  network, broker tie-ups) -> need identification -> Bigul pitch (business platform) ->
  economics (approved structures only) -> support model -> compliance boundary -> **close the
  next step**.
- **Products to know well enough to open the conversation (not be the expert):** trading/
  demat, subscriptions, Mutual Funds, bonds, insurance, Global Invest — each with its
  compliance boundary.
- **Corporate play:** identify connector -> compliant employee awareness/education proposal
  -> HR/admin permission -> run session with approved content + consent-based lead capture
  -> follow up to accounts -> suitability-led cross-sell. Measure by accounts/activation/
  revenue, not attendance.
- **Operating rhythm:** mornings plan + confirm meetings; midday field meetings; late
  afternoon documentation, issue resolution, existing-partner calls; end of day plan
  tomorrow's 5-6 meetings + ready 10 strong follow-ups. Weekly: prospects added, meetings
  done vs planned, registrations + pending docs, 7/15/30 activation, accounts, revenue,
  dormant high-potential, compliance. Monthly: target vs achievement, activation/productivity
  of the month's partners, accounts & revenue per active partner, cross-sell by product,
  territory potential, reactivation plan, next-month strategy.
