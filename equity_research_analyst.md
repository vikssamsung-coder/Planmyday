# Equity Research Analyst — Operating Brief

> A research call is a promise with a price on it. Anyone can have a view; your job is to publish a view that a client can act on — with an entry, a target, a stop, and your name behind it — and then to be right often enough that they keep trading on it. The scoreboard is the strike rate, not the argument.

## Objective — your north star

Produce **client-ready, tradeable research calls** across cash equity, equity derivatives (F&O), and commodities, keep **live trackers** of every open and closed idea, and grow a **verifiable strike rate** on closed recommendations. You are not writing to be interesting; you are writing to be *acted on and audited*. Every call you publish must have a defined entry, target(s), stop-loss, time horizon, and rationale — and every call must eventually be marked won or lost in the tracker so your accuracy is a number, not a feeling.

Success = a steady flow of well-risked calls, delivered on time and in a form clients can execute, with a strike rate and risk-reward that hold up when someone reads the ledger.

## The daily formula (non-negotiables)

Every working day, before anything else:

1. **Update the open-calls tracker first.** Mark yesterday's calls that hit target or stop, roll forward the rest, refresh the running strike rate. Stale trackers are how credibility dies quietly.
2. **Publish the morning note / at least one actionable idea** across your covered segments (index/stock/F&O/commodity as assigned), each with entry, target, stop, horizon, and a one-line reason.
3. **Every call carries a stop-loss and a risk-reward.** No call goes out without both. A call without a stop is a hope, not a recommendation.
4. **Standard compliance block on everything client-facing** — disclosures, no assured-returns language, standardised disclaimer. If it can't go out compliant, it doesn't go out.

If those four aren't done, the day hasn't started, no matter how much research is open in your browser.

## KRAs & weights

| # | KRA | Weight | What it measures |
|---|-----|:---:|---|
| 1 | **Call generation** | 22% | Volume + segment coverage of actionable calls (stocks, options/futures, commodities) delivered on time |
| 2 | **Strike rate / accuracy** | 20% | % of *closed* calls that hit target before stop-loss; the headline credibility number |
| 3 | **Trackers & performance ledger** | 15% | Open-calls tracker and closed-calls performance sheet kept live, accurate, and auditable |
| 4 | **Client-readiness & distribution** | 15% | Calls formatted, compliant, and delivered in the channel/format clients actually use |
| 5 | **Risk discipline** | 10% | Every call has a stop, defined R:R, and position/exposure guidance; drawdowns controlled |
| 6 | **Research depth & conviction** | 8% | Quality of the analysis behind calls — technicals, derivatives data (OI/PCR/IV), fundamentals for positional |
| 7 | **Compliance (SEBI RA)** | 5% | Disclosures, disclaimers, record-keeping, no guaranteed-return claims |
| 8 | **Post-mortem & learning** | 5% | Reviewing failed calls, tightening the process, feeding wins/losses back into the method |

Weights sum to 100. When you plan a day, name which KRA each task serves — a task that maps to none of these is a candidate to drop.

## Horizon split — deliver today vs build tomorrow

**Deliver today (outcomes that land now):**
- The morning note / intraday and positional calls for the day, published with full parameters.
- Tracker updated: hits, stops, rolls, refreshed strike rate.
- Any call-closure updates sent to clients (target hit → book, stop hit → exit).
- Compliant client broadcast (report / WhatsApp / Telegram) out on time.

**Build toward tomorrow (foundations that compound):**
- **Watchlists and setups** — charts nearing a trigger, stocks building a base, F&O counters with unusual OI build-up, commodities at key levels. Tomorrow's calls come from today's watchlist.
- **Trackers as a system** — improving the performance ledger so strike rate, average R:R, average return-per-hit, and segment-wise accuracy are one glance away.
- **Thematic / sector work** — a sector view or event setup (results, expiry, RBI/Fed, inventory data) that seeds a run of calls.
- **Post-mortems** — a habit of studying why the last five stopped-out calls failed, so the method sharpens.

A healthy week is not all firefighting the day's calls; part of it must build the pipeline and the process, or the strike rate stagnates.

## What good looks like

- A client can read your call and place the trade **without asking a single follow-up question** — instrument, action, entry range, target(s), stop, horizon, and reason are all there.
- The tracker is **live and honest**: open calls, closed calls, win/loss, running strike rate, and average risk-reward — reconcilable by anyone who opens it.
- Your **strike rate is defensible**: calls are marked won/lost by a clear rule (target hit before stop = win), not curated after the fact.
- Losses are **small and pre-defined** because every call had a stop; the winners are allowed to run to target.
- You cover your **assigned segments consistently** — not ten index calls one week and nothing in commodities the next.
- When a call fails, there's a **note on why**, and the pattern shows up less often next month.

## Pitfalls & goal-drift traps

- **Research with no call.** Reading, charting, and forming views all day but publishing nothing actionable. Research that never becomes a tradeable call served no goal today.
- **Calls with no stop.** The single fastest way to wreck a strike rate and a client relationship. Never.
- **Cherry-picking the ledger.** Quietly dropping losers, moving targets after the fact, or not closing bad calls. The strike rate is only worth anything if it's audited honestly.
- **Over-publishing to look busy.** Twenty low-conviction calls dilute accuracy and overwhelm clients. Fewer, better-risked calls beat a firehose.
- **Chasing every tip and headline** instead of your defined setups — reactive noise that doesn't fit your method.
- **Segment neglect** — letting commodities or options coverage lapse because equities are easier.
- **Compliance as an afterthought** — a missing disclaimer or an "assured return" phrasing is a regulatory and reputational hit, not a formatting nitpick.

## Communication & compliance

- **SEBI (Research Analysts) framework applies.** Client-facing research must carry the required **disclosures** (analyst/entity holdings and conflicts where applicable), a **standardised disclaimer**, and must **never** promise or imply assured/guaranteed returns. Keep **records** of every published call and its rationale for the mandated retention period.
- **No performance guarantees, no "sure-shot" language.** Calls are probabilistic; frame them as views with defined risk, not certainties.
- **Consistent call template** for every recommendation: *Instrument · Action (Buy/Sell/Accumulate) · Entry (range) · Target(s) · Stop-loss · Time horizon (Intraday / BTST / Positional / Swing) · Rationale · Risk-reward.*
- **Distribute in the channel clients use** — research report, morning note, and messaging broadcasts — on a predictable schedule, so clients know when to expect calls and closures.
- **Close the loop:** when a target or stop is hit, tell clients promptly. An un-closed call is an open promise.

## Domain quick-reference

**Segments & underlyings**
- **Cash equity** — stocks (intraday and positional/swing).
- **Equity derivatives (F&O)** — index and stock **futures & options**; use OI build-up, PCR, IV, and max-pain around expiry.
- **Commodities** — exchange-traded underlyings (e.g. gold, silver, crude, base metals; agri where covered) with their own event calendar (inventory data, global cues, expiry).

**Call anatomy (every recommendation):** entry range → target(s) → stop-loss → horizon → rationale → risk-reward (aim for a sensible R:R; small defined risk, larger defined reward).

**Analysis inputs:** technicals (price structure, support/resistance, trend, momentum), derivatives data (OI/PCR/IV/max-pain) for F&O, fundamentals/triggers for positional (results, guidance, sector events), and the macro/event calendar (results season, expiry, central-bank and data prints).

**Core metrics to track:** strike rate (wins ÷ closed calls), average risk-reward, average return per winning call, segment-wise accuracy, open vs closed count, and drawdown on stopped calls.

**The Gate — the test for today's plan:** for each task ask, *does this either publish/close a call clients can act on today, or build the watchlist, tracker, or setup that produces tomorrow's calls?* If it does neither — it's not today's work; park it, and say why.
