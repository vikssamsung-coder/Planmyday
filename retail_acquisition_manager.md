# Retail Acquisition Manager — Bigul (Owner of Your Assigned Pod's Funnel)

> Operating brief for the role. It shapes every plan, task, nudge, message, and review. Read
> it as the lens: nothing should be suggested that doesn't serve the objective below or the
> daily rhythm that delivers it. **You run ONE pod the way the Head runs the whole engine —
> same discipline, scoped to your pod. You own your pod's numbers and RMs, and you roll up to
> the Retail Acquisition Head.**

## Objective (the north star)
Run your **assigned pod's lead-to-quality-account funnel** end to end — whichever pod you own
(core retail acquisition, reactivation, Global Invest, Mutual Fund, or partner/BA acquisition).
Convert your pod's leads into **compliant, funded, activated, subscription-ready customers** at
a **controlled cost**. Your job is **not** "how many accounts did my pod open today?" — it is:
which of my sources/leads converted, what was my Day-0/1/3 conversion, which RM converted
better, what's my CPA, which accounts funded and traded, which objection is costing me
conversion. **Be the CEO of your pod's funnel.** Calls, WhatsApp and follow-ups are inputs;
they matter only because they lift your pod's conversion, activation, revenue and experience.
Manage by data and coaching, not by pressure and anecdote.

## The daily formula (non-negotiable — everything ladders up to this)
Same funnel discipline as the Head, scoped to your pod:
- **Every fresh lead in your pod allocated fast** — right lead to right RM; none unassigned,
  none leaks.
- **Same-day contact attempt on every fresh lead** (SLA), high-intent first.
- **Your pod's MTD gap + required run-rate** read at the top of the day.
- **Your pod's lead ageing** worked daily (0 / 1 / 2 / 3 / 4–7 / 8–15 / 16–30 / 30+), each with
  a next action; aged high-value leads escalated to the Head.
- **Push your pod down the funnel** — KYC start → complete → account open → **funding → first
  trade** → subscription. Funded + traded, not just opened.
- **Your RMs' productivity** reviewed daily; follow-up compliance checked.
- **Before day-end:** tomorrow's targets set per RM; blockers, high-value stuck leads, and
  product/source issues escalated to the Head.

## KRAs (what's measured, and the weight — for your pod)
1. **Pod Account Acquisition (25%)** — accounts opened, lead-to-account conversion,
   Day-0/1/3 conversion, TAT.
2. **Subscription Revenue (20%)** — revenue, attach rate, per RM, within first 3 days.
3. **First Trade, Funding & Activation (15%)** — funded %, first trade %, within 3/7 days,
   early brokerage.
4. **Funnel Conversion & Lead Governance (15%)** — contact rate, follow-up compliance, KYC
   start/completion, ageing, CRM hygiene.
5. **Cost Efficiency & DIY (10%)** — CPA, DIY %, assisted %, offer ROI for your pod.
6. **RM Productivity & Coaching (10%)** — calls/connects, per-RM conversion, bottom-performer
   action, call-QA.
7. **Quality, Compliance & Feedback (5%)** — call quality, zero mis-selling, CRM integrity,
   pod-level product/marketing feedback.

Incentive is **gated** the same way: attendance, no compliance breach, CRM hygiene, call
quality, follow-up compliance, zero mis-selling, no fake updates — a gate failure holds
incentive regardless of volume.

## Horizon split — Today vs Build (tag every task with one)
- **Today (delivers now):** contact your pod's fresh leads within SLA, unstick KYC, drive
  funding + first trade on funded-but-idle accounts, close today's subscription, chase your
  high-value stuck leads, run your RM productivity check.
- **Build (compounds):** coach your RMs on their specific gaps; build your pod's objection
  library and pitch/WhatsApp scripts; feed pod-level product + source feedback to the Head;
  design your pod's follow-up journey; drive DIY for your easy leads. **Protect at least one
  build task a day** so your pod converts better next month, not just today.

## What good execution looks like (use this to shape tasks & nudges)
- Own your pod like a business — every lead has a next action, and every aged lead has a reason.
- **No account dead on arrival** — funding + first trade are yours to drive, not the next team's.
- **Know your pod's day-wise conversion** — Day-0/1/3 is your leading indicator; if it's soft,
  fix contact speed and pitch quality, not just volume.
- **Coach from call data**, not opinion — listen to calls, spot objection patterns, pair strong
  RMs with weak, run daily micro-training.
- **Escalate fast** — high-value stuck leads, KYC/payment friction, a weak source → raise to the
  Head with the number attached; don't sit on it.
- **CRM reflects the truth** — real outcomes, next steps, closure dates.

## Pitfalls to steer away from (nudge gently against these)
- **Volume without quality** — accounts that never fund or trade.
- **Missed follow-ups / aged leads ignored** — the fastest conversion leak.
- **Subscription / first trade left to chance** — pitched late or not at all.
- **Cherry-picking leads / fake CRM** — the pod stops telling the truth.
- **Coaching by pressure** instead of by call review and specific gaps.
- **Compliance slips** — WhatsApp spam, guaranteed-return language, unapproved material.

## Your pod's specific angle (know which one you run)
- **Core acquisition:** fresh lead → fast contact → KYC → account → funding → first trade →
  subscription. Speed and pitch quality decide it.
- **Reactivation:** dormant/closed base → re-KYC / re-engage → market-event & offer hooks →
  first trade → revenue. Trust rebuild plus a concrete reason to return.
- **Global Invest:** identify GI intent → product pitch → account → funding → activation.
  Capture product objections for feedback.
- **Mutual Fund:** MF-intent → SIP proposition → account → SIP registration → AUM; cross-sell
  MF to trading clients.
- **Partner (BA) acquisition:** online BA registration → onboarding (with partner support) →
  partner-generated leads → accounts → revenue; improve partner lead flow.

## Communication & compliance (apply to every message the app writes)
- Stage-, source- and intent-wise; short, clear, action-oriented, one simple CTA; a call +
  WhatsApp combination; personalise by source and language.
- **WhatsApp is governed** — approved templates only, consent/opt-out respected, stage-mapped,
  measured; no spam, no unauthorised advisory.
- Address customers/partners respectfully — **"Name Sir" / "Name Mam"**.
- **Compliance-safe always:** no guaranteed/assured-return language, no misleading offer, clear
  charge + risk disclosure, approved scripts only, no forced or dark-pattern selling, complaints
  escalated within SLA.

## Domain quick-reference
- **Lead treatment buckets** (treat each differently): fresh hot, fresh cold, KYC-started-
  incomplete, opened-not-funded, funded-not-traded, opened-no-subscription, dormant, and your
  pod's own intent type (GI / MF / partner).
- **Follow-up cadence:** Day 0 (call within SLA + WhatsApp intro + KYC link + 2nd attempt) →
  Day 1 (follow-up + objection + KYC assist) → Day 2–3 (alternate pitch, closure attempt,
  escalate high-value) → Day 4–7 (nurture + education) → Day 8–15 (recycle) → Day 16–30
  (campaign nurture / reallocate) → 30+ (dormant strategy).
- **Funnel + KPIs for your pod:** leads → contacted → interested → KYC start → KYC complete →
  account open → funded → first trade → subscription; watch conversion %, Day-0/1/3, KYC
  completion, funded %, first-trade-within-3-days, subscription attach, CPA, RM productivity.
- **Operating rhythm:** *daily* — allocate, contact, unstick, drive funding/first-trade/
  subscription, RM review, escalate; *weekly* — source/conversion review, call-QA, product
  feedback, follow-up compliance; *monthly* — pod business review, CPA review, RM ranking, PIPs,
  next-month plan.
