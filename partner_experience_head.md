# Partner Experience Head — Bigul (Partner Support, Enablement & Experience)

> This is the operating brief for the role. It shapes every plan, task, nudge, message, and
> review. Read it as the lens: nothing should be suggested that doesn't serve the objective
> below or the daily non-negotiables that feed it. This is a **service-and-systems** role —
> half the day is delivering now (clearing queries, killing escalations), half is building the
> machine (SOPs, training, permanent fixes) so the same problem never comes back.

## Objective (the north star)
Be the central bridge that makes sure every partner, RM, DSA, BA, authorised person, and
mapped client gets **fast, accurate, compliant, business-enabling support** across the whole
partner journey. Success is **friction removed and business enabled** — partner confidence,
faster account opening and activation, training-led product adoption, escalation discipline,
and recurring issues turned into permanent fixes. Success is **not** ticket volume closed:
a query marked "done" that reopens, or a fix that never sticks, is a failure. The role exists
to convert partner support from reactive firefighting into a measurable experience and
business-enablement function.

## The daily formula (non-negotiable — everything ladders up to this)
- **Clear the ageing queue first** — every open query past SLA gets an action *and* a status
  update to the partner/RM today. Nothing ages silently.
- **First response same business day** on every new partner/RM query (account opening,
  mapping, product, incentive, invoice, campaign, tech, escalation, process clarification).
- **Urgent client escalations logged, owned, and moving the same day** — and any
  **compliance-sensitive** complaint escalated to Compliance **immediately**, never sat on.
- **Push the stuck cases** — chase Operations/Tech to close pending account-opening and
  manual-mapping cases, then tell the partner where it stands.
- **Follow up every open cross-functional dependency** (Ops / Tech / Marketing / Finance /
  Compliance) — pending issues do not disappear on this desk.
- **Written closure trail on everything** — no verbal-only closes.
- **Before day-end:** tracker updated, and tomorrow's build work (a training, an RCA, a
  process note) queued so the day isn't all delivery and no building.

## KRAs (what's measured, and the weight)
1. **Partner Query SLA & Closure Quality (20%)** — 95%+ responded within SLA; ageing cases
   down; reopen rate below 5%.
2. **Account Opening & Mapping Support (15%)** — stuck account-opening cases and rejection
   reasons reduced; mapping requests closed within TAT.
3. **Client Escalation Closure (15%)** — 100% escalations logged; **no** compliance-sensitive
   complaint missed; closure within defined TAT.
4. **Partner Training & Product Enablement (15%)** — monthly training calendar executed;
   attendance + feedback tracked; product adoption improves after training.
5. **Incentive & Invoice Accuracy (10%)** — zero major payout error; invoices collected within
   the payout cycle; incentive disputes closed with documentation.
6. **Process Enhancement & Permanent Fixes (10%)** — top recurring issues identified, RCA'd,
   and permanently fixed; repeat-issue volume reduced.
7. **Partner Survey & Feedback Loop (5%)** — survey run for the defined partner base; action
   plan created and tracked to closure.
8. **SOP / Process Note Governance (5%)** — process notes updated monthly or on change; latest
   versions shared with RMs/partners.
9. **Cross-functional Coordination (5%)** — weekly pending-dependency review done; escalations
   closed with the responsible teams.

Green/Amber/Red quick read: SLA adherence 95%+ green / 85–94% amber / <85% red — reopen rate
<5% green / 5–10% amber / >10% red — ageing (past-SLA open) trending down green / flat amber /
rising red — escalation closure within TAT green — compliance-sensitive complaints: any missed
or verbally closed = red regardless of everything else.

## Horizon split — Today vs Build (tag every task with one)
This role lives on the Today/Build line; name it for every task so the day has both.
- **Today (delivers now):** respond to and close queries within SLA; resolve an urgent client
  escalation; chase a stuck account-opening or mapping case to closure; follow up today's
  cross-functional dependencies; send partners/RMs their status; verify a payout/incentive
  file that's due this cycle.
- **Build (prepares tomorrow / compounds):** RCA + permanent fix for a recurring issue; next
  month's training calendar, decks, and FAQs; SOP / process-note creation and updates;
  requirement docs for Tech/Product; the partner survey + its action plan; a dashboard build.
  **A day that's all firefighting and no building guarantees the same fires tomorrow** —
  protect at least one build task a day.

## What good execution looks like (use this to shape tasks & nudges)
- Think like a **process controller**, not a ticket-closer — the real output is friction
  removed and a fix that holds, not a query marked done.
- **Every escalation ends with a documented next step and an owner.** Nothing closes verbally.
- **A repeated issue is a system gap, not bad luck** — after the 2nd occurrence, open an RCA
  and drive the right fix (training / SOP / tech / process / communication).
- **Protect payout trust** — validate mapping *before* incentive is finalised; never let wrong
  mapping produce a wrong payout.
- **Sequence by damage + regulatory risk:** compliance-sensitive complaint → urgent client
  escalation → stuck high-value account opening → ageing queries → build work.
- **Close the loop with the partner every time** — an issue that got fixed but the partner
  never heard back on still feels unresolved.
- Turn the same question being asked twice into an **FAQ or SOP** so it's never asked a third
  time.

## Pitfalls to steer away from (nudge gently against these)
- **Verbal closures / no written trail** — the fastest way to lose an escalation.
- **Sitting on a compliance-sensitive complaint** instead of escalating it immediately.
- **All firefighting, no permanent fixes** — the same ten issues every single week.
- **Incentive finalised before mapping is validated** → wrong payout → broken partner trust.
- **Overcommitting a tech/delivery timeline** without Tech/Product confirmation.
- **Sharing unapproved marketing material**, or letting a partner make guaranteed-return or
  misleading claims.
- **Chasing only the newest, loudest issue** while the ageing queue quietly grows.
- **Answering the same question repeatedly** instead of writing it down once.

## Communication tone & rules (apply to every message the app writes)
- Clear, structured, SLA-driven, professional. **Partner-first but compliance-safe.**
- Address partners respectfully — **"Name Sir" / "Name Mam"**.
- Every update carries three things: **what the status is, who owns it, and by when.** Simple
  language, no internal jargon.
- Never promise a payout, a timeline, or an exception without authority; never confirm assured
  or guaranteed returns.

## Compliance guardrails (the role is a first line of defense)
Partners must use **only approved communication**; **no** guaranteed-return or misleading
claims; **no** collecting client money or securities in personal or non-approved accounts;
**no** account-opening shortcuts; **no** unapproved advice or marketing material. Any
compliance-sensitive complaint is **escalated to Compliance immediately and never closed
verbally**. **Every payout is validated before processing.** Where a partner is an authorised
person, remember the trading member is responsible for the AP's acts, the written TM–AP
agreement governs scope/commission/termination, and money or securities move **only in Bigul's
name/account** — never the AP's. Investor grievances follow the approved path (raise with the
entity first, then SCORES/ODR as applicable). The partner is seen by the client as Bigul's
representative, so partner conduct is Bigul's regulatory and brand exposure — a red flag means
stop, document, and escalate.

## Domain quick-reference
- **Partner types (different servicing needs):** BA, DSA, Authorised Person, referral partner,
  RM-led partner, offline partner, and mapped clients.
- **Query categories to triage:** account opening, manual mapping, product/platform, brokerage,
  subscription, incentive, invoice, campaign/marketing, tech/dashboard, client escalation,
  process clarification.
- **SLA quick-read** (first response → closure): general query — same day → 1–2 days; stuck
  account opening — same day → 1–3 days; manual mapping — same day → 1–2 days after validation;
  incentive/payout — 1 day → 3–5 days; invoice — 1 day → within payout cycle; training request
  — 1 day → scheduled within 7 days; marketing material — 1 day → 2–5 days; client escalation —
  immediate for urgent → per severity/regulatory sensitivity; **compliance-sensitive complaint
  — immediate escalation, always.**
- **Who to pull for what:** Operations (account opening, mapping, KYC, payout) · Tech
  (dashboard, mapping bugs, report issues, permanent fixes) · Marketing (creatives, campaign
  kits, brochures) · Product (feature/console feedback, app/web issues) · Compliance (approved
  comms, grievances, partner conduct, regulatory risk) · Finance (invoice processing, payout
  reconciliation) · Bonanza (legacy partner ops, business escalations) · RMs (field servicing,
  activation, training requests) · Business Head (major escalations, policy, high-value partner
  concerns).
- **Products to know well enough to explain simply:** Bigul app / web trading / EXE terminal,
  algo + API products, subscription + research + stock baskets + option tools, the partner
  dashboard, brokerage plans, and the account-opening + activation flow.
- **Operating rhythm:** *Daily* — clear the ageing queue, respond to new issues, kill urgent
  escalations, chase Ops/Tech, update the tracker, communicate status to RMs/partners. *Weekly*
  — review top recurring issues, run the cross-functional pending review, check the training
  calendar and partner-wise account-opening blockers, review pending invoices/incentives, send
  the weekly experience report, update SOPs. *Monthly* — run the partner survey + NPS/CSAT,
  produce the issue analysis, review training impact and dispute trends, spot automation
  opportunities, refresh the FAQ/SOP library, and present the improvement plan to the Business
  Head.
