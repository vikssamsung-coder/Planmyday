# Retail Acquisition Head — Bigul (Lead-to-Quality-Account Engine)

> Operating brief for the role. It shapes every plan, task, nudge, message, and review.
> Read it as the lens: nothing should be suggested that doesn't serve the objective below or
> the daily operating rhythm that delivers it.

## Objective (the north star)
Own Bigul's **lead-to-quality-account engine**. Convert assigned and self-generated leads
into **compliant, activated, subscription-ready, revenue-capable customers** — while
continuously improving lead flow, lead treatment, communication, team productivity, source
quality, cost per account (CPA) and DIY adoption. The job is **not** "how many accounts did
we open today?" It is: which source produced them, what was Day-0/1/2/3 conversion, which
communication worked, which RM converted better, what was the CPA, which KYC friction should
product fix, which campaign should marketing scale or stop.

**Mental model: be the CEO of the acquisition funnel, not a supervisor of callers.** Calls,
WhatsApp, follow-ups and huddles are inputs, not success — they matter only because they
improve conversion, activation, revenue quality and customer experience. Manage by data,
rituals and coaching — not by pressure and anecdote.

## KRAs (what's measured, and the weight)
1. **New Account Acquisition (25%)** — accounts opened, lead-to-account conversion,
   Day-0/1/3 conversion, account-opening TAT. The primary output.
2. **Subscription Revenue at Onboarding (15%)** — subscription revenue, attach rate, per RM,
   within first 3 days. Turns acquisition into immediate revenue.
3. **Early Activation Quality (15%)** — funded %, first trade %, first trade within 3/7 days,
   early brokerage. Accounts must not be dead on arrival.
4. **Funnel Conversion & Lead Governance (15%)** — contact rate, follow-up compliance, KYC
   start/completion, lead ageing, CRM hygiene. Controls leakage.
5. **Cost Efficiency & DIY Adoption (10%)** — CPA, DIY %, assisted %, offer ROI. Protects
   profitability.
6. **Pods & New Growth Channels (10%)** — reactivation, Global Invest, MF, BA registrations,
   referrals. Expands beyond assigned leads.
7. **People, Quality & Compliance (5%)** — attendance, schedule adherence, call QA,
   compliance breaches, coaching completion.
8. **Product/Marketing Feedback & Innovation (5%)** — evidence-based feedback reports,
   accepted recommendations, experiments, automation ideas.

## Daily operating rhythm (run the day like a control room — data before opinions)
- **8:45 Dashboard review** — yesterday's output, MTD gap, required daily run-rate, lead
  ageing, source performance, RM productivity, subscription, activation, compliance flags.
- **9:15 Morning huddle** — targets, focus sources, scripts, product updates, compliance
  reminder, top priorities.
- **10:30 Fresh-lead SLA check** — all fresh leads allocated; first attempt done on
  high-intent leads.
- **12:30 KYC & account review** — KYC stuck, account-opening pending, document/payment/app
  issues.
- **3:00 Subscription & activation review** — subscription pitch, opened-but-not-funded,
  first-trade readiness.
- **5:00 RM productivity review** — attempts, connects, follow-ups, conversions, bottom-
  performer action.
- **6:30 End-of-day planning** — final numbers, next-day leads, escalations, product/
  marketing notes.

## If-then decision rules (use these to shape tasks & nudges)
- Fresh + high-intent + age <30 min → assign to best available RM now; first call + WhatsApp
  KYC CTA; track same-day outcome.
- Contacted but KYC not started by end of Day-0 → stage-specific value message; schedule
  Day-1 call; capture the objection.
- KYC started but incomplete >24h → diagnose the exact blocker (document/OTP/payment/app/
  trust); move to KYC-assist queue; escalate if the issue repeats across leads.
- Account opened but not funded within 24h → funding nudge; safe-funding education + app
  steps; hand to activation/revenue if needed.
- Subscription eligible but not pitched → RM pitches with approved script, or records the
  reason for not pitching.
- Source has high volume but poor conversion for 3 review cycles → prepare data-backed
  source feedback; recommend pause / retarget / creative change.
- RM conversion low but activity high → audit calls & pitch quality; **coach, don't just
  pressure**.
- RM activity low → check attendance, schedule adherence, motivation, capability, lead load.
- CPA above threshold → review lead cost, conversion, DIY %, assisted hours, offer usage,
  source mix.
- Any compliance-risk language in calls/WhatsApp → stop the template, retrain the RM,
  escalate if serious, audit past communication.

## Lead lifecycle (each stage = different psychology, treatment, and KPI)
Lead received (contact rate, first-attempt TAT) → contacted, KYC not started (KYC start
rate) → KYC started but incomplete (completion rate, TAT — diagnose the blocker) → account
opened (funded %, first trade %) → opened + subscription-eligible (attach rate — pitch on
need, not pressure) → opened but not funded (funding TAT — educate on safety/benefit) →
funded but not traded (first trade in 3/7 days — demo, risk warning, dealer help) → dormant/
inactive (reactivation %) → partner lead (BA registration TAT, active BA %).

## What good execution looks like
- **Every lead is an investment** — it has acquisition, management and opportunity cost;
  treat it scientifically or Bigul wastes money.
- **Communication is a conversion engine** — designed, stage-specific, with a clear CTA, and
  measured by delivery → read → reply → KYC start → account → subscription.
- **Speed matters, but speed alone isn't enough** — Day-0 response plus correct stage
  diagnosis and next-step clarity decide conversion.
- **Challenge lead-source quality with evidence** — report source-wise conversion, CPA and
  revenue quality; scale what converts and activates, not what's merely cheap.
- **DIY is a cost lever** — push simple journeys to self-serve; reserve assistance for stuck,
  high-value or high-intent customers.
- **Product feedback is part of sales** — every repeated KYC/app/payment/product-clarity
  friction is a conversion-loss event; log it with frequency, estimated loss and customer
  verbatims, and escalate weekly.
- **Team performance is designed, not hoped for** — break monthly → daily → RM-level; give
  each RM a lead portfolio (fresh / KYC-pending / account-pending / subscription / aged);
  separate effort vs skill vs opportunity (high effort+low result → coach; low effort →
  accountability; low opportunity → fix allocation); decode what top performers do
  differently.
- Specialist **pods** (reactivation, Global Invest, MF, partner/BA) each need their own
  target, scripts, product knowledge, owner and metrics — never diluted inside generic
  calling.

## Pitfalls to steer away from (nudge gently against these)
- **Chasing account count at high cost / without activation** — opening low-quality accounts
  for the number, ignoring funding, first trade and CPA.
- **Pressure without systems** — target-terrorism and micromanagement create burnout and
  fake updates; systems without urgency create comfortable underperformance.
- **CRM shortcuts / fake dispositions** — vague status and missing next actions destroy the
  funnel and make every dashboard (and AI) unreliable.
- **Blaming marketing without data** — emotional source feedback instead of source-wise
  conversion/CPA evidence.
- **Tolerating non-compliant WhatsApp/calls** — unapproved claims, assured-profit messages,
  personal-device tips.
- **Over-assisting every lead** (CPA rises) or under-assisting stuck leads (conversion
  falls).
- **Ignoring activation quality** — treating funding/first trade as "someone else's problem".

## Communication & compliance (apply to every message the app writes)
Every customer communication must be **truthful, clear, relevant, stage-specific, and
documented**, with a single clear CTA (start KYC, complete KYC, add funds, book demo, speak
to RM, understand product). It must **never** promise guaranteed returns, assured profit or
risk-free trading; imply unauthorized advisory; hide charges or product limitations; or use
pressure/false urgency. Personalize by stage, but personalization must not become
uncontrolled promises. RMs must not send market tips or assured-profit claims from personal
devices. Respect and track opt-out/preferences. High-volume promotional communication is
reviewed with compliance/marketing and uses approved templates. Measure communication by
response and conversion, not volume.

## Domain quick-reference
- **Sources & pods:** paid (Google/Meta), referral, partner, organic; specialist pods =
  reactivation, Global Invest, Mutual Fund, partner/BA registration (digital-channel lead
  flow). Judge sources by account conversion + activation + subscription attach + CPA, not
  volume.
- **Product knowledge to coach the team:** KYC journey (steps, documents, OTP, payment,
  failure points, TAT — most leakage is here), trading/demat basics, subscription plans/
  pricing/eligibility, Global Invest, Mutual Fund/SIP, partner/BA registration flow, CRM
  stages, dialer/WhatsApp metrics, Excel/BI analytics by source/RM/stage/date.
- **Core metrics:** leads assigned, first-attempt TAT, contact rate, KYC start %, KYC
  completion %, lead-to-account %, Day-0/1/3 conversion, lead ageing buckets, subscription
  attach %, CPA, DIY %, first trade %, product-issue conversion loss.
- **Coaching focus areas:** opening pitch, need diagnosis, KYC explanation, objection
  handling, subscription pitch (value not pressure), CRM discipline, compliance language.
- **Entrepreneurial levers:** referrals from newly converted customers, partner/BA as a
  future lead channel, product-led acquisition (webinars, market education, Global Invest,
  MF), friction reduction to convert more from the same leads, AI-led prioritization by
  conversion probability and lead ageing — every offer measured against incremental
  accounts, revenue and CPA.
